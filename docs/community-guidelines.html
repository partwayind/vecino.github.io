1. Purpose of These Guidelines

Vecino — Spanish for "neighbor" — is a structured local services platform connecting Neighbors for real-world contract work: handyman jobs, yard projects, home improvement, cleaning, and similar community-based services. Unlike traditional gig platforms, Vecino is built on transparency, mutual accountability, and a reputation system that both sides earn through completed work.

These Community Guidelines describe how Neighbors are expected to behave on Vecino, what is not allowed, and how we enforce these rules. They apply to every Neighbor, every interaction, and every job — whether you are posting work, offering to do work, messaging another Neighbor, leaving a Vouch, or uploading workspace media.

These Guidelines work alongside our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service. By using Vecino, you agree to follow them.

2. Core Principles

Every rule in this document derives from four principles:

  • Be a good neighbor. Treat the people on the other side of the job the way you would want to be treated by someone working in or near your home.
  • Be honest. About scope, price, timeline, skill level, identity, and what happened on the job.
  • Be accountable. Finish what you agreed to, communicate when things change, and stand behind your work or your post.
  • Keep the platform trustworthy. Vouches, Handshakes, and Job Seals are only meaningful if they reflect real work between real Neighbors. Anything that fakes, bypasses, or gamifies that system harms every other Neighbor on Vecino.

3. Expected Conduct

3.1 Honest Posts and Offers

  • Describe the job accurately. Include real scope, real conditions, and any access or safety considerations.
  • Quote prices you intend to honor. If something changes mid-job, use a Follow-Up Job — not chat, not pressure.
  • Represent your skills honestly. Don't claim trade experience or licensure you don't have.
  • Use real photos. Workspace media should reflect the actual job, not stock images or photos from other work.

3.2 Clear Communication

  • Respond to messages and offers in a reasonable timeframe.
  • If you can't make an agreed time, say so as early as possible.
  • Keep job-related conversation in Vecino's chat so context is preserved if a dispute occurs.

3.3 Following the Closed-Loop Job Flow

Every job moves through seven stages: Post, Negotiate, Locked Agreement, Workspace, Mutual Completion, Job Seal, and Vouch. Each stage exists for a reason — usually to protect both Neighbors. You are expected to:

  • Honor a Locked Agreement once both sides accept it. Mid-job changes go through a Follow-Up Job.
  • Upload accurate workspace media (before, during, after) when you are the working Neighbor.
  • Mark work complete only when it is actually complete.
  • Leave a Vouch after Mutual Completion. Vouching is not optional — it is how the trust system works.

3.4 Respect

  • Speak to other Neighbors respectfully, even during disagreements.
  • Respect the privacy of homes you work in or invite Neighbors into. Don't photograph, share, or discuss what you see beyond what's needed for the job.
  • Respect cultural, religious, and personal differences.

4. Prohibited Conduct

The following behavior is not allowed on Vecino. Engaging in any of it can result in warnings, suspension, or permanent removal — depending on severity.

4.1 Harassment, Discrimination, and Threats

  • Harassment, bullying, intimidation, or threats — in chat, in Vouches, or in person.
  • Discrimination or refusal of service based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, marital status, family status, or any other protected class.
  • Hate speech, slurs, or content that degrades a person or group.
  • Sexual harassment of any kind, including unwanted advances, sexual comments, or requests for sexual content or services.
  • Stalking, doxxing, or sharing another Neighbor's private information without consent.

4.2 Violence and Unsafe Behavior

  • Threats of violence against any Neighbor or their property.
  • Showing up to a job under the influence of alcohol or drugs in a way that affects work or safety.
  • Bringing weapons to a job without prior, explicit agreement (e.g., licensed pest control or wildlife removal).

4.3 Fraud and Deception

  • Impersonating another person, business, or trade professional.
  • Creating multiple accounts to evade soft locks, suspensions, or negative Vouched standing.
  • Posting fake jobs (jobs you don't intend to hire for) or making fake offers (offers you don't intend to honor).
  • Submitting falsified workspace media — staged photos, photos from other jobs, or photos that misrepresent the work performed.
  • Lying about the status of a job, payment, or completion.

4.4 Inappropriate Content

  • Sexually explicit content, nudity, or content that sexualizes minors in any form.
  • Graphic violence or gore.
  • Content that promotes self-harm or suicide.
  • Spam, repeated identical posts, or promotional content unrelated to the job at hand.
  • Malicious links, malware, phishing attempts, or content designed to compromise another Neighbor's account or device.

4.5 Privacy Violations

  • Sharing photos or videos of another Neighbor, their family, or the inside of their home without consent (beyond the workspace media required for the job itself).
  • Posting another Neighbor's contact information, home address, or identifying details publicly or to third parties.
  • Recording audio or video of a Neighbor without their knowledge where prohibited by local law.

5. Prohibited Services and Job Postings

Vecino is a marketplace for lawful, real-world neighborhood services. The following job categories are not permitted, regardless of how they are phrased:

5.1 Illegal Services

  • Any service that is illegal under federal, state, or local law in the location where the work is performed.
  • Drug manufacturing, distribution, or sale.
  • Prostitution, escort services, or any service of a sexual nature.
  • Theft, vandalism, trespassing, or any service that involves harming a third party or their property.

5.2 Services Requiring Licensure You Don't Have

Many jurisdictions require licensure for specific trades. Vecino does not verify trade licensure, but you are responsible for complying with all licensing laws in your area. Do not offer or accept work for trades that legally require licensure you don't hold. Common examples include:

  • Significant electrical work
  • Plumbing modifications to gas, water main, or sewer lines
  • HVAC system installation or refrigerant handling
  • Structural modifications, foundation work, or load-bearing changes
  • Roofing in jurisdictions that require contractor licensure
  • Pest control involving regulated chemicals
  • Asbestos, lead paint, or mold remediation

Small repairs that don't require licensure under local law are fine. When in doubt, check with your local licensing authority.

5.3 Regulated and Restricted Items

  • Firearms, ammunition, explosives, or weapons.
  • Tobacco, vaping products, or cannabis (regardless of state legalization status).
  • Alcohol delivery or service.
  • Prescription medications or medical devices.
  • Live animals, pets, or services involving animal trafficking.

5.4 Services That Endanger Others

  • Childcare, eldercare, or care for vulnerable adults — Vecino does not perform background checks suitable for caregiving roles.
  • Medical services, including any service that involves administering medication or providing medical advice.
  • Transportation of people for hire (use a licensed rideshare service).
  • Food preparation for resale or service to the public outside of your own home.

5.5 Financial Services

  • Loans, money transfers, currency exchange, or any service that resembles a financial product.
  • Tax preparation or financial advising — these require professional credentials and are out of scope.
  • Cryptocurrency, NFTs, or any token-based service.

5.6 Adult Services and Misleading Categories

  • Any "companionship," "massage," or similarly framed posting that is sexual in nature.
  • Posts that use coded language to advertise prohibited services.
If you are unsure whether a service is allowed: ask before posting or offering. Email us at [INSERT CONTACT EMAIL]. It is faster to ask than to have a job removed and your account flagged.

6. Vouch Integrity

The Vouch system — and the Handshake ratings within it — is the foundation of trust on Vecino. Tampering with it harms every other Neighbor. The following are strictly prohibited:

6.1 Buying, Selling, or Trading Vouches

  • Paying for a positive Vouch.
  • Offering discounts, refunds, or services in exchange for a Vouch or specific Handshake rating.
  • Threatening to leave a negative Vouch unless additional money or services are provided.
  • "Vouch swapping" — agreeing to leave each other Vouches without real work being performed.

6.2 Fake Jobs and Self-Dealing

  • Posting and accepting your own job (through a second account or coordinated third party) to inflate Vouched standing.
  • Coordinating with friends or family to run fake jobs through the platform.
  • Creating sock-puppet accounts of any kind.

6.3 Coercive Vouching

  • Pressuring, threatening, or harassing a Neighbor to change, delete, or refrain from leaving a Vouch.
  • Retaliating against a Neighbor who left an honest Vouch by leaving a dishonest one.
  • Using the soft lock system as leverage to extract a positive Vouch.

6.4 Honest Vouches Only

A Vouch must reflect your actual experience with the job. Once submitted, a Vouch is immutable — it cannot be edited, deleted, retracted, or rebutted. Vouches are also permanently blind: you will never see what another Neighbor wrote about you, only the aggregate effect on your Vouched standing. This is intentional and not subject to exception.

Vouch fraud is a permanent-ban offense. The trust system only works if the data behind it is real. Coordinated Vouch manipulation, fake jobs, or buying ratings will result in immediate account termination — for everyone involved — and forfeiture of any pending payouts.

7. Payment and Platform Integrity

7.1 Use Vecino for Vecino Jobs

Every job arranged through Vecino must be paid through Vecino's escrow system. The platform fee (20%, split 10% from each side) funds escrow protection, the Job Seal evidence record, dispute support, and the trust infrastructure that makes the platform work for both sides.

7.2 Off-Platform Circumvention Is Not Allowed

The following are prohibited:

  • Asking another Neighbor to pay you outside of Vecino (cash, Venmo, Zelle, Cash App, wire, etc.) for a job that originated on Vecino.
  • Offering a discount in exchange for paying off-platform.
  • Cancelling a Vecino job with the intent to complete the same job off-platform with the same Neighbor.
  • Sharing personal contact information with the intent of moving a deal off-platform.

Off-platform payment removes escrow protection, eliminates the Job Seal record, and leaves both Neighbors exposed if something goes wrong. It also undermines the trust infrastructure for every other Neighbor on the platform. We monitor for these patterns and act on them.

7.3 Chargebacks and Payment Disputes

  • Don't initiate a chargeback with your bank or card issuer for a completed job. Use Vecino's dispute process instead.
  • Disputes filed in bad faith — for work that was completed and accepted — may result in account suspension and forfeiture of any pending payouts.
  • Job Seals are submitted as evidence to Stripe for any chargeback. The Seal is tamper-evident and cryptographically signed.

7.4 Referrals

Referral earnings are paid based on the first completed job of a referred Neighbor. Self-referral, fake referrals, and coordinated referral schemes designed to extract earnings without genuine new Neighbors are prohibited and will result in payout forfeiture and account termination.

8. Real-World Safety

Vecino jobs happen in the physical world — usually inside or near someone's home. That requires more care than a purely online platform.

8.1 If You Feel Unsafe

  • Trust your instincts. You may decline or leave any job that feels unsafe — before, during, or after starting.
  • If you are in immediate danger, call your local emergency services first. Report to Vecino afterward.
  • You will not be penalized in your Vouched standing for leaving a job due to a credible safety concern. Report it through the in-app reporting flow with as much detail as possible.

8.2 Reasonable Precautions

  • For first-time jobs with a new Neighbor, consider scheduling during daylight hours or letting someone know where you'll be.
  • Review the other Neighbor's Vouched standing — trust tier, risk class, and recent trend — before accepting.
  • Use Vecino's chat for all coordination so the timeline is preserved.

8.3 Property Care

  • Treat the work site with care. Damage caused by negligence or willful behavior is your responsibility and may result in a dispute, payout adjustment, or account action.
  • Do not enter areas of a property you weren't asked to access.
  • Do not bring additional people to a job without prior agreement.

8.4 Children and Pets

  • Do not photograph children or pets in workspace media.
  • If children or pets are present at the work site, the Neighbor at the property is responsible for their safety.

9. Reporting and Blocking

If you encounter behavior that violates these Guidelines, report it. Reports are reviewed by Vecino and can result in warnings, suspensions, or removal of the offending Neighbor.

9.1 In-App Reporting

You can report:

  • A job post, from the post detail screen.
  • A Neighbor, from their profile.
  • A specific message, from the chat thread.
  • A Vouch you believe was left in violation of these Guidelines.

Reports include a category (e.g., harassment, fraud, prohibited service, off-platform circumvention, safety concern) and free-text details. Provide as much specificity as possible — screenshots, message references, and dates.

9.2 Blocking

You can block another Neighbor from a profile or chat thread. Once blocked, that Neighbor will no longer be able to message you, see your posts, or appear in your Locals view. Blocking is not the same as reporting — block to protect yourself, report to flag a violation for review.

9.3 Confidentiality

Reports are confidential. We do not disclose the identity of the reporting Neighbor to the reported Neighbor. We may, however, contact you for additional context.

9.4 Emergencies

If you are in immediate danger or witnessing a crime in progress, contact your local emergency services first. Vecino is not an emergency service and cannot respond in real time. After you are safe, report the incident to us so we can take account-level action.

10. Enforcement and Consequences

We review reports and act based on severity, frequency, and the impact on other Neighbors. Possible actions include:

Action When We Use It
Warning First-time, minor, or ambiguous violations
Content removal Specific posts, messages, or media that violate these Guidelines
Feature restriction Temporary loss of posting, offering, or messaging privileges
Temporary suspension Repeated minor violations or a single moderate violation
Permanent ban Severe violations: fraud, Vouch manipulation, harassment, threats, illegal services, safety incidents, or repeated suspensions
Payout forfeiture Where the violation directly relates to money earned through fraud or platform circumvention

Account actions are not appealable in real time — they are designed to take effect immediately to protect other Neighbors. You may submit an appeal after the fact (see Section 13).

Vouched standing — trust tier, risk class, recent trend — also reflects your behavior on the platform. Repeated incomplete jobs, ghosting, or active dispute flags lower your standing automatically. These are not punitive moderation actions; they are factual reflections of your job history.

11. Soft Locks and Outstanding Vouches

Vouching is mandatory for the trust system to work. If you fail to leave a Vouch after a completed job, your account will be soft-locked:

  • You will be unable to post new jobs and unable to make new offers, regardless of which role you held in the outstanding job.
  • The lock applies in both directions — it is not limited to the role where you failed to vouch.
  • The lock is released automatically once you submit the outstanding Vouch.

Soft locks are not a punishment. They exist because the trust system depends on every completed job producing a Vouch. Skipping it doesn't just affect you — it leaves the other Neighbor's reputation incomplete.

Using the soft lock as leverage — for example, threatening to withhold a Vouch unless the other Neighbor agrees to something — is a violation of these Guidelines and may result in account action.

12. Disputes

If something goes wrong on a job, Vecino provides a structured dispute process. The Job Seal — a cryptographically signed, tamper-evident archive of the job's scope, messages, workspace media, and payment record — is the primary evidence record.

12.1 What Vecino Does

  • Holds funds in escrow until the dispute is resolved.
  • Provides the Job Seal as evidence.
  • Facilitates communication between the two Neighbors where appropriate.

12.2 What Vecino Does Not Do

  • Vecino does not adjudicate disputes. Where payment disputes escalate to chargebacks, they are resolved by Stripe based on submitted evidence.
  • Vecino does not provide legal advice or substitute for the courts.

12.3 Dispute Flags

Disputes generate flags on the involved Neighbors' profiles. Flags are never deleted — only their status changes between active and neutralized. Active flags weigh against your Vouched standing; neutralized flags do not. The makes_it_right badge is awarded when a working Neighbor resolves a dispute and its flags are neutralized.

12.4 Bad-Faith Disputes

Filing a dispute in bad faith — for work that was completed and confirmed, or to extract a refund after consuming the service — is a violation of these Guidelines and may result in account action and forfeiture of any pending payouts.
