If you have access to the app

The fastest and easiest way to delete your Vecino account is directly inside the app:

  1. Open the Vecino app.
  2. Go to Settings.
  3. Tap Delete my account.
  4. Confirm when prompted.

Your account immediately enters a 30-day recovery window. During this window, you can change your mind by logging back in — you will be given the option to recover your account or confirm deletion.

If you do not log back in within 30 days, the deletion is finalized and your account cannot be restored. See the Privacy Policy §6.2 for the full retention and scrub policy.

If you cannot access the app

If you have uninstalled the app, lost access to your device, or are otherwise unable to use the in-app flow, you can request deletion by email.

Email: [INSERT CONTACT EMAIL]
Subject line: Account Deletion Request

In the body of your email, please include:

  • The email address you registered your Vecino account with.
  • A brief note stating that you want your account deleted.

We will process your request within 30 days of receiving it, consistent with our Privacy Policy §6.2 commitment.

Verification: To protect against malicious deletion attempts, we may ask you for additional verification before honoring the request. We will follow up by email if we need anything from you.

What happens after deletion

Once the 30-day recovery window closes (or immediately upon a confirmed email request, once processed), we scrub your account as follows:

What is removed

  • Profile contents: your username, avatar, encrypted contact information, payment-method references, and encryption keys are cleared.
  • Auth email: your registered email address is replaced with a non-deliverable tombstone in the authentication system, so it cannot be used to log in or receive mail.
  • Push notification binding: your device's push notification token is detached.

What is preserved

The following records are retained because they are legally required or are load-bearing for other neighbors' records:

  • Job offers and accepted agreements.
  • Payment logs and referral payouts.
  • Job Seal archives (cryptographically signed, tamper-evident records of completed work).
  • Chat messages.
  • Vouches you wrote.

These records remain intact, but anywhere your name or avatar would have appeared — in job histories, chat threads, Seal archives, and vouches — the platform will display "Deleted Neighbor" in place of your identity.

For the complete retention schedule, see the Privacy Policy §6.2.
